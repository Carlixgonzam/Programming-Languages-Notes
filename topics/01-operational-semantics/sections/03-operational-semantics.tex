\subsection{Big-step semantics}
In \emph{big-step} (natural semantics, a judgement) $\bigstep{e}{\sigma}{v}$ asserts that expression $e$ in store $\sigma$ evaluates \emph{directly} to value $v$, without exposing intermediate steps. Derivations are trees whose leaves are axioms and whose internal nodes are inference rules.

\subsubsection{Lists: \texttt{concat} and \texttt{append}}

We assume the constructors $\nil$ (empty list) and $\cons{h}{t}$ (list with
head $h$ and tail $t$).

\paragraph{\texttt{concat}$(e,\ell)$, insert element $e$ at the end of $\ell$.}

\[
\infer[\textsc{Concat-Nil}]
  {\bigstep{\concat{e}{\nil}}{\sigma}{\cons{e}{\nil}}}
  {}
\qquad
\infer[\textsc{Concat-Cons}]
  {\bigstep{\concat{e}{\cons{h}{t}}}{\sigma}{\cons{h}{t'}}}
  {\bigstep{\concat{e}{t}}{\sigma}{t'}}
\]

\paragraph{\texttt{append}$(\ell_1, \ell_2)$ concatenate two lists.}

\[
\infer[\textsc{Append-Nil}]
  {\bigstep{\append{\nil}{\ell_2}}{\sigma}{\ell_2}}
  {}
\qquad
\infer[\textsc{Append-Cons}]
  {\bigstep{\append{\cons{h}{t_1}}{\ell_2}}{\sigma}{\cons{h}{\ell'}}}
  {\bigstep{\append{t_1}{\ell_2}}{\sigma}{\ell'}}
\]

Derivation for append
We verify $\bigstep{\append{\cons{1}{\nil}}{\cons{2}{\nil}}}{\sigma}{\cons{1}{\cons{2}{\nil}}}$:

\[
\infer[\textsc{Append-Cons}]
  {\bigstep{\append{\cons{1}{\nil}}{\cons{2}{\nil}}}{\sigma}{\cons{1}{\cons{2}{\nil}}}}
  {
    \infer[\textsc{Append-Nil}]
      {\bigstep{\append{\nil}{\cons{2}{\nil}}}{\sigma}{\cons{2}{\nil}}}
      {}
  }
\]
\subsection{Small-step semantics}

In \emph{small-step} semantics, a judgement
$\steppair{P}{\sigma}{P'}{\sigma'}$ performs a single atomic reduction.
A program is fully evaluated when no rule applies (it is a value or a
stuck state) or when it reduces to $\mathbf{skip}$ (for commands).

\subsubsection{For-loops in SIMP}

The grammar of SIMP extended with \textbf{for} is:

\[
C ::= \assign{l}{E} \mid \seq{C}{C} \mid \ift{B}{C}{C} \mid
      \whiledo{B}{C} \mid \mathbf{skip} \mid \fordoo{C}{B}{C}{C}
\]

The cleanest approach is to treat \textbf{for} as \emph{syntactic sugar}:

\[
\fordoo{C_i}{B}{C_s}{C_b}
\;\triangleq\;
\seq{C_i}{\whiledo{B}{\seq{C_b}{C_s}}}
\]

If the language requires explicit small-step rules (no sugar), we give:

\[
\infer[\textsc{For-Init}]
  {\steppair{\fordoo{C_i}{B}{C_s}{C_b}}{\sigma}
            {\fordoo{C_i'}{B}{C_s}{C_b}}{\sigma'}}
  {\steppair{C_i}{\sigma}{C_i'}{\sigma'}}
\]

\[
\infer[\textsc{For-Skip}]
  {\steppair{\fordoo{\mathbf{skip}}{B}{C_s}{C_b}}{\sigma}
            {\ift{B}{(\seq{C_b}{\seq{C_s}{\fordoo{\mathbf{skip}}{B}{C_s}{C_b}}})}{{\mathbf{skip}}}}{\sigma}}
  {}
\]

Rule \textsc{For-Init} reduces the initialiser one step at a time.
Rule \textsc{For-Skip} fires once the initialiser has been fully executed:
it checks the guard and either runs the body followed by the step expression
(and loops), or terminates with $\mathbf{skip}$.
